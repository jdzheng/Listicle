THREE MUST TRY FOODS IN ROWLAND HEIGHTS

Jerry Zheng
Last updated Apr 18, 2017

1. "Buttload of Fries" at Tastea

1737 Fullerton Rd,Rowland Heights, CA

Crunchy and perfectly seasoned. These Buttload of Fries are a must have if you find yourself in Rowland Height's famous "Yes Plaza." These fries have a secret seasoning that even the workers themselves don't know. There is initial sweet taste followed by a salty after taste along with a mixture of other spices. Even more so, the crunchy outer coating creates a nice composition with the soft potato. Although the potato flavor may be masked by the seasoning, the overal taste cannot be matched by any other late night snack food place.

2. "Milky Bun" at Afters

18910 Gale Ave, Rowland Heights, CA

You may have seen donut ice-cream sandwiches at the dining halls, but nothing compares to the "Milky Bun" that they serve at Afters. Originally opened in Irvine, CA, the ice-cream shop recently opened up a branch in Rowland just a year ago. They serve a variety of interesting flavors from Thai Tea and Vietnamese Coffee to Cookie Monster and Churro. These unique flavors placed between warm, fluffy and freshly glazed donuts offer the perfect dessert or afternoon snack. You truly do leave your diet there.

3. KBBQ at Kang Ho Dong Baekjeong

18900 Gale Ave, Rowland Heights, CA

If you're looking for quality Korean BBQ then you've found the right place. Kang Ho Dong Baekjeong offers a unique KBBQ dining experience as opposed to other locations in Rowland Heights. Rather than an all-you-can-eat menu, Kang Ho Dong Baekjeong only offers an order by plate option. Although more expensive than typical KBBQ restuarants, the quality of meat is definitely unbeatable. You won't regret your decision once you see the marbling on the rib-eye. Did I mention, that they also have corn-cheese on the side, along with steamed-egg and a variety of delicious ban-chan!
